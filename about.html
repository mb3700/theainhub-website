The founding gift

Named for Mark Ain.

In 2023, Mark Ain—co‑founder of Kronos—committed a $2M gift to establish the Ain Hub within FGCU’s Daveler & Kauanui School of Entrepreneurship, to give students real‑world experience by serving real clients.

“I was using my extra time and effort looking for an idea around which to invent a product and start a company.”Mark Ain — on the years before founding Kronos

That same spirit drives the Hub today: students gain real experience serving clients while keeping their eyes open for the opportunities that could become their own ventures.

Who runs it

Led by someone who’s done it.

Founder and Faculty Fellow Mark Bole has taught AI at FGCU since 2023. Before that: corporate executive, co‑founder of an international investment firm in Luxembourg—launched straight into the 2008 crisis and survived by protecting clients’ money—and franchise CEO.

His pattern: “every job I ever had, I didn’t know how to do when I got it—you learn by doing.” That’s exactly how the Hub’s students learn, and how we help your team. A practitioner, not a pundit.

“Make AI understandable, show where it fits in real business, and measure the impact.”Mark Bole — founder
How we got here

From websites to workshops to integration.

The Ain Hub opened in 2024 with five students, doing what we could teach immediately—websites and online marketing for local businesses, effectively an in‑house student agency.

As it became clear businesses needed to learn to use AI, we built hands‑on workshops—growing from eight people in a room with no projector to a 185‑person seminar. Advisor and community leader Brian Rist opened the door to the Sanibel community, where we ran workshops for local businesses.

Then AI shifted from “write my emails” to building real solutions—and so did we. Today we do full AI integration: capturing workflows, automating the busywork, and building the internal tools teams actually use.

Student‑powered

A feature, not a discount.

Mark oversees every engagement. Our students work right alongside your domain experts—they bring current AI fluency, your people bring the expertise that makes it useful. It goes both ways, and the capability stays with you.

Where we’re headed: students as full “agents of change,” placed inside companies to drive adoption. That’s the vision we’re building toward—our way of helping Southwest Florida adapt to AI.
