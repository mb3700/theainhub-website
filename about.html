About

Built at FGCU. Powered by students.

We help Southwest Florida businesses put AI to work—not in theory, in their actual workflows. Faculty‑led, student‑powered, and based at Florida Gulf Coast University.

Our story

From websites to workshops to AI integration.

The Ain Hub started in 2024 with five students and a $2M founding gift. We began with what we could teach immediately—websites and online marketing for local businesses, effectively an in‑house student agency.

As it became clear that businesses didn’t know how to use AI, we built hands‑on workshops—and grew from eight people in a room with no projector to a 185‑person seminar and 40 businesses in a single session.

Then AI shifted from “write my emails” to building real solutions. Today we do full AI integration—embedding students inside companies to capture workflows, automate the busywork, and build the internal tools their teams actually use.

The founding gift

Named for Mark Ain.

The Hub was established with a $2M gift from Mark Ain, co‑founder of Kronos, within FGCU’s Daveler & Kauanui School of Entrepreneurship—to give students real‑world experience by serving real clients.

“I was using my extra time and effort looking for an idea around which to invent a product and start a company.”Mark Ain — on the years before founding Kronos

That same spirit drives the Ain Hub today: students gain real experience serving clients while keeping their eyes open for the opportunities that could become their own ventures.

Who leads it

Faculty‑led—by someone who’s done it.

Faculty Fellow Mark Bole has taught AI at FGCU since 2023. Before that: corporate executive, co‑founder of an international investment firm in Luxembourg—launched into the 2008 crisis and survived by protecting clients’ money—and franchise CEO.

His pattern: “every job I ever had, I didn’t know how to do when I got it—you learn by doing.” That’s exactly how the Hub’s students learn, and how we help your team.

“Make AI understandable, show where it fits in real business, and measure the impact.”Mark Bole — Faculty Fellow
Agents of change

Student‑powered is a feature, not a discount.

Our students don’t just assist—they walk into a company and help on day one. We call them agents of change. They bring fresh, current AI fluency; you get capability that stays in‑house long after the engagement ends.

It’s a mission, not just a margin: we’re helping Southwest Florida adapt to AI—and our students are the answer.
