What it is

We build the thing itself.

Integration is where AI stops being a tool a few people poke at and becomes part of how the work gets done. We build a working automation and show the hours it gives back, then leave your team able to run it.

How we do it

Capture. Build. Prove. Spread.

Four steps, weeks not years, with a human in the loop the whole way.

1

Capture

We record an expert doing the real work (30 to 60 min) and turn it into a knowledge base and a reusable skill.

2

Build

We build it into Claude, and into internal apps with Replit and Vercel, then test and refine it before automating.

3

Prove

A working automation and hours back, in weeks. The thing itself.

4

Spread

We start with one team of 3 to 5 and turn them into champions. Then we keep going (training the next group, and the next), building champions across every department, with the capability staying in‑house.

Proof · Manufacturing

An hour a day, back, times 33 reps.

A fast‑growing regional manufacturer. We started with sales (33 reps) and recorded the best rep’s hour of pre‑consult prep.

Before
The best rep’s playbook lived in one person’s head, and every rep spent about an hour a day assembling their own prep by hand.
After
An automated daily brief for all 33 reps, CRM + county property‑appraiser data + owner research → talking points and dos & don’ts. About an hour a day, back per rep.
Capacity returned, in the language a CEO actually cares about.A manufacturing engagement
  • Recorded the top rep’s 1‑hour prep on a screen‑share
  • Turned it into a knowledge base + a reusable skill
  • Automated a daily brief that runs for every rep
  • Freed the team’s mornings for selling
  • ~1 hour/day of selling capacity returned, per rep
