The problem

Two problems, every time.

Every company we walk into has the same two: information stuck in silos and inboxes, and good people drowning in the busywork of moving it around. Underneath it, you know you need to start with AI, but not where, and you can’t bet the company on a guess. You don’t have to.

How we work

We don’t hand you a deck. We build the thing.

Four stages, with your team’s judgment in the loop the whole way, and a real deliverable at each one, not a roadmap.

1

Align

Start with your leadership team in the room; agree where AI fits and what “good” looks like.

2

One team, 3 to 5 people. A workshop to get them over the fear, or straight at one real workflow.

3

4

Spread

We start with one team of 3 to 5 and turn them into champions. Then we keep going, training group after group, until champions reach every department, with the capability staying in‑house.

Where we go deepest
How we actually build it

The same method, every engagement.

There’s no mystery to it. It’s exactly how we work ourselves. We record an expert doing the real work, turn it into something reusable, build it into the tools your team already opens, then teach your people to run and extend it.

A human stays in the loop at every step. The goal isn’t to replace anyone’s judgment. It’s to take the busywork off their plate.

  • Record an expert doing the work (30 to 60 min screen‑share)
  • Turn it into a knowledge base + a reusable skill
  • Build it into Claude, and into internal apps with Replit / Vercel
  • Test, refine, and automate. Your team keeps the judgment
  • Teach your people to run it, and extend it themselves

The research layer

Every engagement makes the next one sharper.

We don’t just build and leave. Every project (and every semester of students shipping real work) teaches us something about how teams actually decide with AI. We turn that into frameworks and playbooks that feed straight back into the work.

1

Build

Real decision systems for real businesses, each one a live experiment in what actually works.

2

Measure

We learn which decisions AI improves, which it degrades, and where human judgment is decisive.

3

Teach

The findings become frameworks and curriculum, so every client and every student gets the benefit.

A competitor can copy any single project. Nobody can copy the loop between building, measuring, and teaching. It compounds with every engagement.
